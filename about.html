Why Comp140 Recipes are Flawed

The common justification for recipes is that they force you think in terms of math and logic instead of a specific programming language. In theory this a great idea, but in practice, the format of a "well written" recipe is so strict and inconvenient to use that no student I have talked to has ever written their recipe before their code. If recipes truly aim to help students think about their code, they should be graded less strictly. Tedious formatting rules such as not allowing equal-sign variable assignment and requiring subscript indexing should be removed. I understand this opens up the possibility for students to write hand-wavy pseudocode that oversimplifies complex algorithms. However, this is preferable to what students already do, which is to code in python first anyways, then tailor a recipe based on their python code.
